# Crypto Pitch Case Studies

Real fundraise analysis showing what worked and why. Use these as benchmarks and inspiration — not templates to copy.

**Sources:** TechCrunch, protocol blogs, FasterCapital case studies, public pitch analysis.

## Phantom — $1.2B Valuation, $109M Total Raised

**Rounds:** Series A ($9M, a16z), Series B ($109M at $1.2B, Paradigm), Series C ($150M at $3B, Sequoia + Paradigm)

### What Made It Work

| Element | What Phantom Did | Why It Worked |
|---------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Traction slide** | 40K → 2.1M users in 6 months. 100K new users/week. | Growth rate was the entire pitch. Numbers spoke for themselves. |
| **Market positioning** | "90% of Solana wallet usage" | Owned the category. Not "we're a wallet" but "we ARE the Solana wallet." |
| **Why Solana** | Positioned as "the UX layer" for Solana's speed/cost advantages | Didn't pitch Solana to VCs — pitched that Solana users need great UX and they're the only ones delivering it. |
| **Multi-chain vision** | "We'll expand to Ethereum and Polygon" | Showed a path from Solana-dominant to multi-chain without abandoning their base. |
| **Demo** | Working product with real users. Not a mockup. | Investors could install it and try it during the meeting. |

### Lessons to Apply
- **If you have explosive growth, lead with it.** Don't bury metrics on slide 8. Make them slide 2.
- **Own a category, don't compete in one.** "The Solana wallet" > "A wallet for Solana"
- **Show the product working.** Phantom's demo was "install our Chrome extension right now."

## Uniswap — $11M Series A (a16z, Paradigm, USV)

### What Made It Work

| Element | What Uniswap Did | Why It Worked |
|---------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Network effects** | More liquidity → more traders → more liquidity | Classic platform dynamics. VCs love flywheels. |
| **Technical innovation** | AMM model, flash swaps, governance tokens | Not just iterating — fundamentally new primitive. |
| **Traction** | Trading volume, liquidity depth, user growth all up and to the right | Multiple metrics reinforcing the same story. |
| **Open source** | Fully open, composable, forkable | Counterintuitive: being forkable proved the protocol was a standard, not just a product. |

### Lessons to Apply
- **Show the flywheel.** If your product has network effects, make that a dedicated slide with a clear diagram.
- **Multiple metrics > one metric.** Volume + liquidity + users = a story. Just volume = a number.
- **Open source as moat.** In crypto, being the standard that others build on IS the moat.

## Filecoin — $257M ICO (One of the largest token raises ever)

### What Made It Work

| Element | What Filecoin Did | Why It Worked |
|---------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Ecosystem proof** | 400K+ participants, 3K+ developers | Massive community before launch proved demand. |
| **Hard metrics** | 2.5 exabytes of storage on mainnet | Infrastructure projects need to prove they work at scale. |
| **Social impact** | "Decentralized storage for humanity" | Combined profit motive with mission. |
| **Technical credibility** | Protocol Labs team, IPFS track record | Team had already shipped a widely-used protocol. |

### Lessons to Apply
- **Infrastructure pitches need scale proof.** "It works" isn't enough — "it works at X scale" is.
- **Team credibility matters more for infrastructure.** Previous shipping experience is table stakes.
- **Community as traction.** For token launches, community size and engagement ARE the traction metric.

## Axie Infinity — $7.5M Series A (Mark Cuban, Alexis Ohanian, Animoca)

### What Made It Work

| Element | What Axie Did | Why It Worked |
|---------|-------------|---------------|
| **Engagement depth** | 1.5M DAU, 250K Discord, 100K Twitch viewers | Not just signups — daily active usage across multiple platforms. |
| **Product-market fit proof** | Users earning real income in Philippines | The "aha" moment: people playing a game to pay rent. |
| **Retention > acquisition** | Focused on DAU/MAU ratio, not total downloads | Showed sticky engagement, not viral-then-dead pattern. |

### Lessons to Apply
- **Engagement > downloads.** DAU/MAU ratio is more impressive than total signups.
- **Real-world impact stories.** "Users earn $X/month" beats "we have X users."
- **Show where your users actually are.** Discord, Twitch, Twitter — prove community is active, not just big.

## Jupiter — Dominant Solana DEX Aggregator

### What Made It Work

| Element | What Jupiter Did | Why It Worked |
|---------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Volume dominance** | Largest DEX aggregator on Solana by volume | Category definition through metrics. |
| **Community-first** | JUP token airdrop to actual users | Aligned community incentives with growth. |
| **Product breadth** | Aggregator → limit orders → DCA → perpetuals | Showed expansion from wedge to platform. |
| **Developer ecosystem** | Open APIs used by hundreds of protocols | Other projects depending on you = platform lock-in. |

### Lessons to Apply
- **Wedge → platform narrative.** Start with one thing, show the expansion path.
- **Developer adoption as moat.** "200 protocols use our API" is stickier than "200K users."
- **Community alignment.** Airdrop to actual users (not speculators) builds defensible community.

## Common Patterns Across All Successful Pitches

### What Every Winner Had
1. **Traction first, vision second.** Working product with real metrics > grand future vision
2. **Category ownership.** "THE Solana wallet" not "A Solana wallet"
3. **Network effects or flywheel.** Some mechanism that gets stronger with scale
4. **Team credibility.** Prior shipping experience in the domain
5. **Honest about what's missing.** Acknowledged gaps rather than hiding them

### What No Winner Did
1. Led with tokenomics
2. Used top-down TAM ("crypto is a $2T market")
3. Claimed "no competitors"
4. Showed only vanity metrics
5. Relied on future promises without present proof

### Traction Benchmarks by Stage

| Stage | What VCs Expect (2025-2026) |
|-------|---------------------------|
| **Pre-seed** | Working prototype, 50-500 users or waitlist of 1000+, founder-market fit story |
| **Seed** | 1K-10K active users, some retention signal, early revenue or clear path |
| **Series A** | 10K-100K MAU, 20%+ MoM growth, clear unit economics, retention curve flattening |
| **Growth** | 100K+ MAU, revenue, clear path to profitability, expanding TAM |

### On-Chain Metrics That Matter (Solana-specific)

| Metric | Why It Matters | How to Show It |
|--------|---------------|----------------|
| **Daily Active Wallets** | Real usage, not bots | Link to Dune/Flipside dashboard |
| **Transaction Count** | Product is being used | Solana Explorer program page |
| **TVL** | Economic commitment | DefiLlama protocol page |
| **Retention (D7, D30)** | Stickiness | Cohort chart from on-chain data |
| **Revenue** | Sustainability | Protocol fee accumulation address |
| **Composability** | Platform potential | Number of protocols integrating with you |
