## Specific Patterns Preserved

These concrete patterns from the article are represented in the references:

- contextual trays instead of unnecessary full-screen flows
- titles and orientation controls on transient surfaces
- tray height variation to clarify progression
- context-aware theming
- onboarding transitions that reveal route structure
- directional tab motion
- icon morphs during forward/back movement
- button label morphs for semantic change
- continuity of existing components across steps
- transaction spinners moving to their destination surface
- reward moments after backup/security actions
- hidden playful interactions in medium-frequency surfaces
- stronger delight intensity for infrequent features

## Intended Usage

Use the references as applied design guidance, not as historical commentary. The goal is to reproduce the philosophy and decision rules, not the exact Family UI.

## What This Package Deliberately Does Not Copy

This package avoids reproducing the article's image-heavy demonstrations and long-form prose. Instead it converts them into:

- reusable decision rules
- generalized patterns
- audit questions
- implementation-facing heuristics

That keeps the skill operational while still covering the article's substance.
