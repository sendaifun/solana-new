# Family Values Review Checklist

Use this checklist when auditing a flow, screen, or product area.

## Simplicity

- Are only the immediately relevant controls visible?
- Is the first step small and approachable?
- Are edge cases deferred until they matter?
- Does each step have one dominant action or idea?
- Would a contextual surface preserve understanding better than a full-screen jump?
- Is the user always oriented by clear titles, hierarchy, and back/dismiss behavior?

## Fluidity

- Can the user see how one state became the next?
- Does motion communicate direction, hierarchy, or consequence?
- Do persistent elements keep their identity across transitions?
- Are meaningful text or icon changes acknowledged rather than swapped abruptly?
- Does the flow avoid teleportation between disconnected states?
- In high-trust moments, does the transition increase confidence?

## Delight

- Is the baseline polish consistently high across common and uncommon surfaces?
- Is delight strongest where frequency is lowest or emotional importance is highest?
- Are common tasks kept efficient and restrained?
- Does surprise feel optional and safe?
- Are rewards used to mark important completions?
- Would this still feel good after repeated use?

## Product Integrity

- Are utility, performance, and security still being treated as non-negotiable?
- Did polish improve the structure, or is it covering up weak structure?
- Do neglected corners make the product feel less cared for overall?
- Were trade-offs made intentionally rather than accidentally?

## Quick Scoring

Score each principle from 1 to 5:

- **1**: actively harmful
- **2**: weak and inconsistent
- **3**: competent but generic
- **4**: strong and intentional
- **5**: distinctive and systemically coherent

If one category scores below 3, fix that before layering more expression on top.
